History and Governance

AidOpener’s history and governance


AidOpener is not yet an organization: it is still only a starter concept and website. The website is owned and run by an individual, Michael Medley. When trying to research the impact of aid in South Sudan, Michael began writing a computer program in mid-2014 for more convenient access to data from the International Aid Transparency Initiative (IATI). That program has became the first version of the IATI Aid Activity Tabulator. The website – and the concept of AidOpener – has begun being built around that. The site was first uploaded in April 2015.

AidOpener so far has no material resources other than those of Michael as a volunteer activist. The idea is now to develop it into a more formal project, and to give it strength and stability by setting it within a foundation or other regulated organization. AidOpener will report all its funding and project activities on this website.

Michael Medley’s work has centered largely on Sudan and South Sudan, where he has been a teacher, aid-worker, researcher and consultant, beginning in 1987. He gained a PhD from Bristol University (UK), studying the politics of humanitarian aid. He now lives in Chiang Mai, Thailand, and is the regular guest co-ordinator of the course on Development Project Management for the MA in International Development Studies at Chulalongkorn University.
